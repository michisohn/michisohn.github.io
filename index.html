»» Master Thesis: Serialization Strategies for Relational Data in Bi-Encoder Dense Retrieval ««
(preliminary results)

Comparing six serialization strategies (labeled fields, JSON, Markdown, HTML, instruction-style, LLM summary) for embedding structured records in a content-based recommender, evaluated across two embedding models (OpenAI text-embedding-3-small, Google text-multilingual-embedding-002), two retrieval modes, and 24 LLM-judged eval cases on NDCG@5, MRR, Precision@5, Hit Rate@5, and latency.

LLM-summarized inputs lead in Profile Mode with OpenAI (NDCG@5 0.958, Precision@5 0.875), while instruction-style serialization wins with Google. Differences between strategies are small at the top of the ranking but compound on Precision@5. HTML serialization is consistently the fastest at no measurable quality cost. Query Mode underperforms Profile Mode across all configurations, suggesting short queries don't carry enough signal for dense retrieval alone.
